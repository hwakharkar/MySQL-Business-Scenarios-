# Day 1 - Understanding the Sakila Database

## Objective

The objective of this exercise is to explore the Sakila database, understand its structure, and identify the relationships between key business entities. This forms the foundation for writing SQL queries that answer real business questions.

---

## Business Scenario

As a Solution Consultant, one of the first tasks when working with a new customer is to understand their data model before proposing reports, integrations, or technical solutions.

The Sakila database represents a DVD rental business. Before building reports or integrating with external systems, it is important to identify where customer, rental, payment, inventory, and film information is stored.

---

## Business Questions

This exercise answers the following questions:

- What tables are available in the database?
- How many customers are registered?
- Which customers are active and inactive?
- Where is film information stored?
- Where are rental transactions stored?
- Where are payment records stored?
- Which tables represent stores and staff?
